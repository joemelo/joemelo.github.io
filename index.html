Subordinate Clauses

Subordinate Clause - a clause, typically introduced by a conjunction, that forms part of and is dependent on a main clause. A subordinate clause (or dependent clause) is a clause that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence because it does not express a complete thought.
Dependent Clauses- A dependent clause is a group of words with a subject and a verb. It does not express a complete thought so it is not a sentence and can't stand alone.
